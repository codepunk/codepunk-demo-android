Thoughts:
In onFling:
scrollableX is the total amount the image can be scrolled horizontally. Always >= 0
scrolledX is the current amount the image is scrolled horizontally. Always >= 0* (Unless we're portraying overScroll)
startX is the X-coordinate of the touch event
startX + scrolledX is how far to the right of startX you would be able to scroll.
startX + scrolledX - scrollableX is how far to the left of startX you would be able to scroll.
In other words, scrolledX is the amount you could scroll to the right, and scrolledX - scrollableX is the amount you could scroll to the left.
If the image bounces back towards the left a little bit at the end of flinging to show the left-hand side of the image (i.e. flinging in the right-hand direction), that would mean ... our max isn't big enough.
If the image bounces back towards the right a little bit at the end of flinging to show the right-hand side of the image (i.e. flinging in the left-hand direction), that would mean our min is too big.

In the above example we ended up 22 pixels off.
scrollableX is 3708
scrolledX (i.e. amount we can scroll TO THE RIGHT) is 1293
scrolledX - scrollableX (i.e. amount we can scroll TO THE LEFT) is -2415
